Building with

I’m Harshal Nikose — entrepreneur, Rotary Club member, Member of MACCIA, and founder of Lumicure Pharma Pvt. Ltd. My work is rooted in a simple belief: quality healthcare should be accessible to every Indian, especially the elderly and underserved.

Lumicure operates an asset-light pharma model, sourcing high-quality medicines from CDMOs under our own brand. Driven to make healthcare more humane through the distribution of long-term medicines commonly prescribed for elders, with a strong focus on availability, continuity of treatment, and patient trust.

Caring for my elders taught me that medicines are not just pills, but responsibility. What began with my family and neighbours is now Golilo — helping elders manage medicines with dignity and ease.

02 — The Story

Where it all
began

My journey into healthcare didn’t start with a business plan. It started with a simple problem at home.

While helping my Papa and Nani with their daily medicines, I noticed something obvious yet ignored — too many strips, unclear instructions, similar-looking tablets, and complete dependence on others. Medicine existed, but clarity didn’t.

The tension wasn’t dramatic. It was subtle and everyday.

Repeated questions. Missed doses. Quiet anxiety.

Medication had become complicated, when it should have been simple.

Instead of adding more tools or complexity, I chose reduction. I organised medicines by routine and time. Removed confusion. Created order. Nothing fancy — just clarity.

That small change worked.

I then extended the same approach to 20–25 elders in my society. No apps. No noise. Just a system that respected their habits and independence. The result was calm, confidence, and fewer mistakes.

This thinking became Golilo — not a product, but a philosophy:

Good care doesn’t need decoration. It needs intention.

The same belief shapes my work at Lumicure Pharma. We focus on ethical medicines, dependable systems, and unnecessary cost removed — so quality can speak for itself.

Today, I work at the intersection of pharma, systems, and empathy, guided by one principle:

“When you remove what is unnecessary, what remains becomes meaningful.”

If this way of thinking resonates with you, I’m always open to a quiet, thoughtful conversation.
